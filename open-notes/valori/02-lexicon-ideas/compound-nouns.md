# Compound Nouns (Exploratory)

## Observation

Nouns are currently:
- palindromic cores (aVa, eYe, etc.)
- or derived structures (arOva, oRro)

---

## Expansion Direction

Compound nouns may emerge from:
- consonant interaction
- layered structures
- domain embedding

---

## Example Direction

```

trak

```

→ structured object derived from:
t + r interaction within a domain

---

## Key Idea

Nouns are not labels.
They are structural descriptions.

---

## Potential Model

Simple → palindromic
Complex → compositional

---

## Constraint

A valid noun must:
- be reducible
- not introduce new primitives
- remain interpretable from structure

---

## Risk

If compounds become memorized:
→ system becomes lexical, not generative

---

## Open Questions

- What distinguishes noun vs verb structurally?
- Can any structure act as a noun?
- Do nouns require closure (palindromic or terminal)?
