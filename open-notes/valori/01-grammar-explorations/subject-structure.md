# Subject Structure (Exploratory)

## Observation

Subjects in Varoli are not entities. They are perspectives.

The vowel alone defines the subject:
- e → self
- a → collective
- o → other
- i → all
- u → between

---

## Current State

Subjects appear as standalone tokens preceding the verb:

```

e vi
a vi

```

This is sufficient for:
- minimal sentences
- perspective shifts

---

## Open Direction

Subjects may evolve beyond single vowels into structured expressions:

```

e vo oRro

```

→ self individuating as friend

---

## Key Question

Is the subject:
- a fixed position?
- or simply the first declared perspective?

---

## Risk

Introducing subject structure (e.g. nesting or grouping) may require:
- scope rules
- precedence rules

These do not currently exist.

---

## Constraint

Subjects must remain:
- derivable from primitives
- structurally interpretable without external grammar

---

## Open Questions

- Can subjects be multi-layered without grouping syntax?
- Is “subject” even a necessary category long-term?
- Can perspective shift mid-expression?
