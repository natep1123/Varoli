## Resolution-Unit Chaining — Provisional Structural Note (Consolidated)

**Status:** Provisional. This note captures the working chaining architecture derived from iterative testing against the existing language system. It supersedes the earlier chaining note. It is not canon closure. Weaker elements are explicitly marked.

---

### 1. What Has Been Established

The native compositional unit of expression in Varoli is the resolution unit: a structurally bounded composition of primitives whose terminal element determines whether the unit resolves locally, projects force beyond its boundary, or suspends in superposition.

The smallest validated resolution unit is `V + CV + VCV`: a perspective marker, a directional verb, and a stable palindromic noun.

Multi-unit chaining does not require a connector mechanism. Chaining behavior emerges from the structural properties of terminal elements and the interaction between adjacent units. No new primitives or mechanics are introduced. The chaining architecture uses only: palindromic closure, binary directionality, the containment relationship, and the operator's superposition-collapse function.

---

### 2. Terminal Behavior

The terminal element of a resolution unit determines the unit's state. Three states are currently identified.

**Close.** Terminal element is a stable centered form (VCV palindrome). The palindromic noun absorbs the preceding verb's directional force through containment. The unit resolves locally. No force is projected. Strongly supported in all single-verb cases tested. Multi-verb unit closure remains untested.

**Project.** Terminal element is a directional form (CV or VC). The unit emits directed force beyond its boundary. No stable domain exists within the unit to absorb the force. The unit is structurally open. Strongly supported across all cases tested, including domain-consistent forms, operator verbs, and both CV and VC terminals. No directional terminal tested resolves internally.

**Suspend.** Terminal element is the operator (y). The unit is held in superposition at its terminal. The operator marks the position as unresolved. The unit neither closes nor projects. Strongly supported across all cases tested. Terminal operator consistently suspends.

---

### 3. Cross-Unit Interaction Model

When a projecting unit is followed by another unit, the interaction is determined by what the projected force encounters. The provisional chaining architecture is best modeled as two governed interactions and one default state.

#### Resolution (project into close)

Projected force from a projecting unit resolves in the first stable domain (palindromic noun) it reaches in a subsequent closing unit. The governing mechanism is the containment relationship: kinetic force operates within substrate domain.

The strongest current hypothesis is that the containment relationship can govern reception regardless of compositional origin, with cross-boundary verification still weaker than within-form verification. Within-form precedent supports multi-force containment (`arOva` contains two kinetic forces within one substrate domain; the soul crucible contains multiple forces within nested domains). The architecture defines containment by role class and function without a provenance condition. No primitive, rule, or role class encodes origin-sensitivity.

The strongest current deep-reception hypothesis is that projected force is not received by the framing element and instead reaches the first structurally compatible stable domain. Whether the subject marker is transparent, receives, or blocks incoming force is undeterminable from current testing. Deep reception bypasses this ambiguity by identifying the noun as the structurally compatible receiver.

The verification gap: within-form multi-force containment is confirmable through palindromic closure. Cross-boundary multi-force containment is not confirmable through the same mechanism. The architecture currently favors the relationship by definition while leaving its cross-boundary confirmation weaker than the within-form case.

#### Suspension (project into suspend)

Projected force from a projecting unit encounters a subsequent unit whose terminal is operator-marked. The subsequent unit contains no stable domain. The projected force has no reception target under the deep reception rule.

The current architecture leans toward extended suspension: the terminal operator encompasses both the unit's own unresolved force and the incoming projected force, producing a composite suspended state. This lean is based on the architecture's provenance-blindness (no origin marker exists, no rule conditions operator scope on compositional history) and the operator's canonical description ("acts on relationships between primitives" without restriction to compositionally native primitives).

The counter-reading (operator scope is limited to the unit boundary) would require either provenance-sensitivity or unit-boundary-as-scope-boundary, neither of which has been formalized.

Extended suspension is provisionally supportable but is the weakest supported interaction in the chaining architecture. Operator scope in cross-boundary contexts has not been formally derived or independently verified.

#### Continuation (project into project)

Projected force from a projecting unit encounters a subsequent unit that also projects. The subsequent unit contains no stable domain and no operator. The projected force has neither a reception target nor a suspending mechanism.

Continuation is currently the least assumption-laden default state when no receiver or suspender is encountered. The projected force persists. It is not resolved. It is not suspended. It continues forward, seeking a stable domain or operator in whatever follows.

The subsequent projecting unit generates its own directed force. Both forces — the incoming projected force and the newly generated force — continue forward together. Their coexistence is not prohibited by any current rule. Their inter-relationship is not specified by any current mechanism. They are co-present and both seeking resolution.

Continuation is not a governed interaction in the same sense as resolution and suspension. It is the absence of the triggering conditions for those two interactions. It does not require a positive mechanism. It is the structural state of unreceived projection persisting.

The chain remains open until a stable domain closes it or an operator suspends it.

### 4. What Inter-Force Governance Currently Adds

When two directional forces resolve within the same stable domain (through deep reception in a closing unit), their relationship is provisionally differentiated by orientation.

**Opposite-orientation forces (one CV, one VC).** The directional asymmetry produces a structurally readable relationship: one force arrives in the domain while the other departs from it. The current architecture supports deriving a more determinate relation from the CV/VC distinction alone. This is binary directionality applied to two forces within a shared space. The chain specifies down to the relational layer.

**Same-orientation forces (both CV or both VC).** Same-orientation chains are currently the best candidate for relational openness within the tested chaining architecture. The forces are identifiable by their different invariant functions and perspectives, but the inter-force relationship is not specified by orientation. The forces are co-present and co-directional within the shared domain. The chain specifies down to the domain layer and leaves the relational layer open.

Both types are meaningful. Neither is currently shown invalid within the tested chaining architecture. The distinction is in determinacy of the inter-force layer, not presence or absence of meaning. This is the first strongly plausible native multi-unit distinction derived from existing directional architecture.

---

### 5. What Is Strong vs What Remains Weaker

**Strong:**

Terminal behavior (close/project/suspend) is strongly supported across all tested cases. Each terminal state is determined by the structural type of the terminal element. No exceptions found.

Continuation as default is strongly supported by the architecture's existing commitments. No rule prohibits unreceived projection from persisting. The default state requires no positive mechanism.

Inter-force directional governance (opposite vs same orientation) is strongly supported by the binary directionality principle applied to multi-force compositions.

**Provisionally supported:**

Deep reception (projected force resolving in a subsequent unit's stable domain) is provisionally supported by architectural definition and within-form precedent, with cross-boundary verification weaker than within-form verification.

**Weaker:**

Extended suspension (terminal operator encompassing incoming projected force) is the weakest supported interaction. It is favored by provenance-blindness and the operator's canonical description but not independently verified. Operator scope in cross-boundary contexts is not formally derived.

Subject marker behavior at unit boundaries (transparent, blocking, or dual-role) is undeterminable from current testing.

Multi-verb unit closure (can one noun absorb more than one verb within the same unit) is untested.

### 6. What Minimal Tested Forms Currently Support the Note

**Terminal closure:** `e ve oRo` — stable terminal absorbs verb. Unit closes.

**Terminal projection:** `e ve` — directional terminal. Unit projects.

**Terminal suspension:** `e ve y` — operator terminal. Unit suspends.

**Deep reception:** `e ve` | `a va oRo` — projected force provisionally received by subsequent unit's noun. Both forces resolve within the mountain.

**Opposite-orientation governance:** `e ve` | `a av oRo` — CV arrival meets VC departure within shared domain. Relationally determinate.

**Same-orientation co-presence:** `e ve` | `a va oRo` — two CV forces within shared domain. Co-present, relationally open.

**Accumulated suspension:** `e ve` | `a va y` — projected force encounters suspended unit. Provisionally encompassed by terminal operator.

**Continuation:** `e ve` | `a va` — projected force encounters projecting unit. Neither resolves nor suspends. Both forces continue.

### 7. What Open Questions Remain

Can same-orientation inter-force relationships be resolved by additional structural mechanisms, or is relational openness their characteristic condition?

Can chains of more than two units follow the same rules? Does a third unit's force resolve, suspend, or continue under the same interaction model?

Does continuation accumulate without limit, or is there a structural constraint on how many unresolved forces can coexist?

Can multi-verb units close under a single stable terminal?

How does suspension collapse? What structural context resolves a suspended chain? Does it require a stable domain arriving later, or can other mechanisms trigger collapse?

How does typography extend to chained forms?

Does the architecture need to explicitly formalize the containment relationship as a derived law to support chaining at larger scales?

What is the operator's scope in extended and cross-boundary compositions? This is the single outstanding question whose resolution would most strengthen the chaining architecture.

---

### 8. What the Next Bounded Development Question Should Be

The chaining architecture now has a provisional interaction model with two governed types and one default. The model operates on two-unit chains. The next question is whether the model holds at three units.

The smallest three-unit test would chain projection through continuation into resolution:

`e ve` | `a va` | `i li oRo`

Unit 1 projects. Unit 2 projects (continuation). Unit 3 closes. The question: do both accumulated projected forces (`ve` from Unit 1 and `va` from Unit 2) resolve within Unit 3's stable domain (`oRo`)?

If yes: continuation feeds into resolution cleanly. The chain model scales from two units to three. Multiple units of continuation can precede a single closing unit, with all accumulated forces resolving at the point of closure.

If no: continuation does not feed into resolution, and the model has a scaling limitation.

This is the smallest test that pressures the interaction model beyond pairwise chaining. It asks whether the two-unit results compose into a three-unit chain under the same rules.
