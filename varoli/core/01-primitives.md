# 01 — Primitives

Varoli is built on three primitive classes: vowels, consonants, and operators. This is not an arbitrary classification. Each class serves a fundamentally different role in the structure of meaning.

Vowels define the elemental substrates of the system. Consonants define forces acting within those domains. Operators act on the structure itself, governing how elements resolve without introducing new content.

No class can be reduced to another. A vowel cannot behave like a consonant. A consonant cannot behave like an operator. An operator cannot become an element or a force. The separation is absolute.

All valid forms in the language arise from the interaction of these three classes. If their roles are preserved, the system remains stable. If their roles are blurred, the system collapses.

---

## The Three Categories

### Vowels

Vowels are elemental. Each vowel represents a specific element and a specific perspective of conscious experience. They are the *where* of reality: the substrates that meaning exists within.

```
a   e   i   o   u
```

Vowels are never interchangeable. Each one occupies a fixed position in the ontology of the language. Meaning does not transfer between vowel categories.

---

### Consonants

Consonants are kinetic. Each consonant represents a specific quality of motion or relationship. They are the *how* of reality: the forces that make elements tangible and directional.

The consonant set is divided into two subsets:

**Attractor Consonants (4)**

These four consonants each belong to a specific element. They are the quality that makes that element real rather than abstract. Every elemental palindrome is centered on its attractor consonant.

```
v   l   r   m
```

| Consonant | Element | Role |
|-----------|---------|------|
| v | Water (a) | Vitality: the force that makes water flow with intention |
| l | Air (i) | Continuum: the force that makes breath persist |
| r | Earth (o) | Resonance: the force that makes land vibrate with weight |
| m | Void (u) | Depth: the force that makes voidlight hum from the interior |

Fire (e) has no attractor consonant. Its center is the operator. See: Operators.

**General Consonants (17)**

These consonants do not belong to any single element. They are applied forces that move between elements, across them, and through them.

```
g   w   th   c   p   b   ch   k   f   s   h   t   d   n   sh   j   z
```

---

### Operators

Operators modify or determine how relationships between primitives resolve. They do not carry elemental weight or directional bias of their own.

```
y
```

There is currently one operator in the language. Additional operators may be introduced only if they describe a genuinely new class of function not expressible through vowels or consonants.

**Notes:**
- `th`, `ch`, and `sh` are digraphs treated as single characters
- `y` is counted once despite appearing across multiple functional contexts
- `c` and `k` represent the same threshold quality at different intensities: `c` is the soft crossing, `k` is the hard crossing

## The Hard Rules

These rules govern the entire language system. They are not guidelines. They do not have exceptions.

**Rule 1: Operators do not carry elemental or relational meaning.**
`y` does not belong to any element. It does not describe a quality of motion. It modifies or determines how elements and relations resolve. It is the event, not the substance.

**Rule 2: Function is invariant.**
Every character has a fixed function. That function defines its role in all valid constructions and cannot be altered by context, interpretation, or poetic usage.

**Rule 3: Interpretation cannot override function.**
Every character also has a poetic field: an embodied, felt-sense interpretation that makes the function humanly transmittable. The poetic field is subordinate to the function. If a poetic reading conflicts with the function, the function wins.

**Rule 4: The attractor state of any valid word must be determinable unambiguously from its structure.**
The capital letter in the typographic system marks the attractor state. This is not stylistic. It is the system identifying its own center. If the attractor state of a word cannot be determined unambiguously, the word is not yet valid.

## Fire and the Operator

Fire (*e*) is the only element without an attractor consonant. This is not a gap. It is the most important structural fact in the system.

The four other elements resolve through a quality of motion: water through vitality, earth through resonance, air through continuum, void through depth. They become tangible through what moves through them.

Fire does not resolve through a quality of motion. Fire resolves through the event of collapse itself. Self-awareness is not made real by something moving through it. It is made real by the moment it recognizes itself. That moment is `y`. The operator. The becoming.

The elemental palindrome of fire is therefore:

**eYe**

Fire, operator, fire. The operator at the center. Fire made real through the moment of collapse. The only palindrome in the language whose center is not a consonant quality but the event that determines all qualities.

## The Typographic System

Varoli uses capitalization to mark the attractor state of a word: the structural core the form resolves around. In simple forms, this is a single character. In more complex forms, it may extend beyond a single letter. The full typographic system is still being formalized alongside the morphemic layer.

- **aVa** — water eternal. V is the attractor. Palindromic balance visible at a glance.
- **arOva** — island. O is the attractor. Earth at the center of water.
- **Varoli** — the spoken tongue. O is the attractor. The grounded thing.
- **Yemu** — the unspoken tongue. Y is the attractor and the operator. The collapse at the center of depth and fire.
- **eYe** — fire eternal. Y is the attractor. The operator at the center of fire.

The typographic rule applies to all words in Varoli. Every word shows its center. Every center is its attractor state. The eye finds the truth of the word before the mind reads it.

The attractor state must always be determinable unambiguously from structure. The capital letter is not a choice. It is the system proving itself.
