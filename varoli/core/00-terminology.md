# 00 — Terminology

This document defines the canonical terminology used across Varoli.  
These terms are binding within the structural layer of the system.

They must be used consistently in all definitions, rules, and schema.  
Descriptive language may vary outside the structural layer, but must not contradict these terms.

---

## Core Ontological Terms

| Term | Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Substrate** | The elemental domain within which structure exists. | Used for vowels. Preferred over “layer” or “plane.” |
| **Domain** | The functional space defined by a substrate. | Often interchangeable with substrate in context. |
| **Function** | The invariant structural role of a primitive. | Cannot change with context. |
| **Primitive** | A fundamental unit of the system that cannot be reduced further. | Vowels, consonants, operator. |

---

## Structural Terms

| Term | Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Structure** | A valid arrangement of primitives. | Must follow generation rules. |
| **Form** | A realized structure (word or construction). | Used in both generation and interpretation contexts. |
| **Composition** | The process of combining primitives into structure. | Must be derivable. |
| **Reduction** | The process of resolving a structure into its primitive components. | Used to validate forms. |

---

## Vowel-Specific Terms

| Term | Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Substrate** | The ontological domain defined by a vowel. | Primary descriptor of vowels. |
| **Register** | The experiential or identity layer associated with the substrate. | Descriptive, not structural. |
| **Kinetic Tendency** | The natural directional behavior of the substrate. | Supports interpretation. |

---

## Consonant-Specific Terms

| Term | Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Kinetic** | The quality of motion a consonant represents. | Primary descriptor of consonants. |
| **Directional Bias** | The natural vector of a consonant’s motion. | Inward, outward, static, threshold. |
| **Interaction** | The effect of a consonant acting within a substrate. | Must remain compositional. |

---

## Operator Terms

| Term | Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Operator** | A structural mechanism that acts on relationships between primitives. | Not a substrate or force. |
| **Superposition** | A state in which a position is unresolved. | Marked by `y`. |
| **Collapse** | The resolution of an unresolved position through context. | Must be structurally determined. |
| **Resolution** | The process by which meaning becomes determined. | Applies across the system. |

---

## Attractor and Resolution Terms

| Term | Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Attractor** | The central state a structure resolves around. | Determines center-outward reading. |
| **Attractor State** | The resolved center of a structure. | Often marked typographically. |
| **Center-Outward Reading** | Interpreting structure from attractor outward. | Core interpretation method. |

---

## Generation and Interpretation Terms

| Term | Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Generation** | The construction of valid forms from primitives. | Governed by generation rules. |
| **Interpretation** | The reading of meaning from structure. | Governed by interpretation rules. |
| **Derivable** | A structure that can be fully reduced to primitives. | Required for validity. |
| **Non-derivable** | A structure that cannot be reduced to primitives. | Invalid unless marked as exploratory. |

---

## Prohibited or Non-Canonical Terms (Structural Layer)

The following terms should not be used in formal definitions:

| Term | Reason |
|------|--------|
| “Layer of existence” | Too interpretive. Use **substrate**. |
| “Plane” | Ambiguous. Use **domain** or **substrate**. |
| “Transform” | Implies domain change. Consonants do not change substrates. |
| “Ambiguity” | Misrepresents operator behavior. Use **unresolved state** or **superposition**. |
| “Meaning shift” | Function does not shift. Use **scale realization** if needed. |

---

## Usage Rule

- Structural documents must use canonical terminology.
- Descriptive prose may vary, but must:
  - not redefine these terms
  - not contradict their definitions

If a new term is needed:
- it must be defined here before being used structurally.
