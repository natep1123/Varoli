# 03 — Consonants

Quick reference. For full definitions see `./05-phonemic-schemas.md`.

Consonants define the forces of the system. They are not objects or entities, but qualities of motion: the ways in which something acts, changes, constrains, or propagates within a domain.

Each consonant represents a distinct quality of motion. These qualities are compositional and directional. When combined with vowels, they produce structured meaning through the interaction of force and domain.

Consonants do not carry meaning independently of vowels. They require a domain in which to operate. Their role is to shape, direct, or express what the vowel makes possible.

If vowels define where existence occurs, consonants define how it behaves.

---

## Core-Attractor Set

These four consonants carry elemental affinity. They make their paired element real rather than abstract.

---

### v — Vitality
**Function:** Self-originating outward projection. Force that initiates from an internal source rather than as a result of external cause.
**Bias:** Outward
**Affinity:** Water (a)
**Poetic:** The current. The wake behind the canoe.

---

### l — Continuum
**Function:** Bidirectional threading force. Maintained connection across a span. Persistence across distance and time.
**Bias:** Bidirectional, permeating
**Affinity:** Air (i)
**Poetic:** The horizon line. The navigation thread.

---

### r — Resonance
**Function:** Internally oscillating force. Vibration within a bounded form that radiates outward from that vibration.
**Bias:** Static with internal propagation
**Affinity:** Earth (o)
**Poetic:** The echo in stone. The vibration felt through the soles before it is heard.

---

### m — Depth
**Function:** Inward descending force. Movement toward interior states, hidden layers, and conditions beneath the surface of what is perceptible.
**Bias:** Inward, descending
**Affinity:** Void (u)
**Poetic:** The trench. The closed-mouth hum. The weight of deep water from all directions.

## General Set

These seventeen consonants carry no elemental affinity. They are applied forces that move between elements, across them, and through them.

---

### The Threshold Pair

#### c — Soft Threshold
**Function:** Gentle crossing force. Transition that parts or crosses a boundary without rupture, moving with rather than against.
**Bias:** Threshold (laminar)
**Poetic:** The leaf cutting the surface of water. The door that opens because you know how to approach it.

---

#### k — Hard Threshold
**Function:** Forceful crossing force. Transition that ruptures or severs a boundary through impact rather than alignment.
**Bias:** Threshold (turbulent)
**Poetic:** The hull striking the reef. The hard stop before something new begins.

---

### The Pressure Pair

#### p — Compression
**Function:** Inward compressive force. Presses toward a center, adding weight or density without releasing.
**Bias:** Inward, downward
**Poetic:** The anchor. The weight that holds position before it gives way.

---

#### b — Emergence
**Function:** Outward expansive force. Pushes through resistance from a compressed state into expression.
**Bias:** Outward
**Poetic:** The bubble rising. The bloom breaking through soil.

---

### The Impact Pair

#### t — Contact
**Function:** Pointed contact force. A single precise moment of connection between two things without continuation.
**Bias:** Static, pointed
**Poetic:** The step. The exact moment of landing.

---

#### d — Resistance
**Function:** Opposing contact force. Meets an incoming force and holds its ground rather than yielding.
**Bias:** Inward, opposing
**Poetic:** The shield. The levee. The sensation of something that does not give way.

---

### The Flow Set

#### s — Seeking
**Function:** Linear outward probing force. Extends from a source toward a target through a medium, searching for a path or resonant match.
**Bias:** Outward, linear
**Poetic:** The sonar pulse. The hiss of something searching.

---

#### z — Recognition
**Function:** Resonant receiving force. Vibrates in response to an incoming probe, signaling that the target has been found.
**Bias:** Inward, static
**Poetic:** The bell struck. The vibration that says: here.

---

#### h — Boundary
**Function:** Containing boundary force. Defines the limit between inside and outside, holding what is within while negotiating contact with what is without.
**Bias:** Surrounding
**Poetic:** The canoe hull. The skin. The thin layer that makes inside possible.

---

### The Union

#### n — Cohesion
**Function:** Static binding force. Holds parts together in a stable configuration, preventing separation without actively pulling toward a center.
**Bias:** Static, binding
**Poetic:** The knot. The rope under tension.

---

### The Elemental Tools

#### w — Oscillation
**Function:** Cyclic bidirectional force. Moves in a repeating pattern between two states without permanently arriving at either.
**Bias:** Bidirectional, cyclic
**Poetic:** The wave. The rhythm of water meeting land and pulling back.

---

#### th — Dissolution
**Function:** Dispersing permeating force. Breaks a structure down by penetrating it from within and releasing the cohesion that held it together.
**Bias:** Permeating, dispersing
**Poetic:** The mist. The salt in water. The slow erasure of edges.

---

#### g — Accumulation
**Function:** Inward convergent gathering force. Draws dispersed things toward a center without necessarily binding them.
**Bias:** Inward, convergent
**Poetic:** The net. The harvest. The tide pool filling.

---

#### f — Release
**Function:** Outward dispersing release force. Allows contained pressure to escape gradually without rupture.
**Bias:** Outward, dispersing
**Poetic:** The foam. The sigh of something that has been held too long.

---

### The Friction Pair

#### ch — Abrasion
**Function:** Lateral bidirectional friction force. Generated when two forces move against each other along a shared surface, producing heat or wear through sustained contact.
**Bias:** Lateral, bidirectional
**Poetic:** The sand. The grindstone. The heat of two surfaces that will not yield.

---

#### j — Catalyst
**Function:** Sudden threshold-crossing release force. Accumulated tension crosses a threshold and releases as a sudden outward burst.
**Bias:** Threshold, outward
**Poetic:** The spark gap. The spring released. The moment abrasion becomes ignition.

---

#### sh — Dampening
**Function:** Permeating static dampening force. Reduces the amplitude of other forces, softening impacts and absorbing energy without redirecting it.
**Bias:** Permeating, static
**Poetic:** The ash. The rain on still water. The quality of silence after a loud event.
