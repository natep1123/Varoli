# 02 — Vowels

Quick reference. For full definitions see `05-phonemic-schemas.md`.

Vowels define the elemental substrates of the system. They are not symbols or categories, but the ontological conditions within which anything can appear, relate, differentiate, or stabilize.

Each vowel represents a distinct and non-overlapping domain. These domains are invariant. They do not shift based on context or interpretation. All meaning in the language ultimately resolves through them.

Every structure in the language is anchored in a vowel domain. The vowel does not describe the structure. It is the condition the structure exists within.

Where consonants are the kinetic forces of the system, vowels define the substrates within which those forces act. Every structure in Varoli is anchored in a vowel, and the vowel determines the fundamental nature of that structure.

Vowels are the substrate of meaning itself.

---

## Vowel Set

### u — Voidlight

**Function:** Undifferentiated origin. The substrate from which distinction can arise. Independent of relation, form, individuation, and field.

**Poetic:** Pressure without location. The silence that precedes sound rather than follows it.

---

### i — Air

**Function:** The condition in which appearance occurs. The structural prerequisite for anything to be perceived or related. Present wherever something appears without being the thing that appears.

**Poetic:** The invisible. The breath already present before the decision to breathe.

---

### a — Water

**Function:** Relational resonance. The medium through which distinct things affect, connect, and dissolve into each other. Relation itself, not the field that contains it.

**Poetic:** The tide. The sensation of being surrounded by something that receives without choosing.

---

### e — Fire

**Function:** Individuation. The process by which something becomes distinct and maintains itself as a bounded identity separate from the field and from relation.

**Poetic:** The crackle before the flame. The instant something becomes distinct from everything around it.

---

### o — Earth

**Function:** Stable, addressable form. That which is bounded, identifiable, and can be referenced as a specific thing. That which can be pointed to.

**Poetic:** The weight of stone underfoot. The cold and density of something that holds its shape.
