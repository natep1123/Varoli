# 01 — Primitive Verbs

Primitive verbs are CV structures in which a force (consonant) expresses into an elemental substrate (vowel). They do not describe actions upon objects. They describe modes of existence or states of being within a given element.

Every primitive verb answers one question: how does this force exist within this element?

The CV direction is primary. Force projects into element. The VC inversion (element projecting through force) is the reception layer and is documented separately.

This document covers the v-set only: the five modes of self-originating projection across the five elemental substrates. Additional attractor sets (l, r, m) will be documented as the system expands.

For the function and poetic definitions of each primitive, see `02-vowels.md` and `03-consonants.md`. For generation rules governing valid CV structures, see `06-generation-rules.md`.

## The v-Set: Self-Originating Projection

*v* = self-originating outward projection. Force that initiates from an internal source rather than as a result of external cause.

Each entry below describes what it means for self-originating projection to express into a specific elemental domain.

---

## va

**Structure:** v + a

**Function:**
Self-originating projection into relational resonance. Existence expressed through connection, interaction, and flow. To project outward into the medium through which distinct things affect each other.

**Poetic Field:**
Being-as-relation. The self moving outward into the current. Existence felt as participation in flow rather than as a bounded thing standing apart from it.

---

## ve

**Structure:** v + e

**Function:**
Self-originating projection into individuation. Existence expressed as the active process of becoming and maintaining a distinct bounded identity. To project outward as the force that makes something itself rather than part of a field or relation.

**Poetic Field:**
Being-as-self. The crackle of something declaring its edges. Existence felt as the ongoing act of remaining distinct.

---

## vi

**Structure:** v + i

**Function:**
Self-originating projection into the condition of appearance. Existence expressed as contribution to the universal field in which things can be perceived or related.

**Poetic Field:**
Being-as-presence. The wake left in still water. Existence felt as what the field receives when something passes through it.

---

## vo

**Structure:** v + o

**Function:**
Self-originating projection into stable addressable form. Existence expressed as becoming a specific, addressable form.

**Poetic Field:**
Being-as-form. The moment something settles into its shape. Existence felt as the process of becoming solid enough to be located.

---

## vu

**Structure:** v + u

**Function:**
Self-originating projection into undifferentiated origin. Existence expressed as movement toward or emergence from the substrate prior to distinction. To project outward into the domain that precedes all form, relation, individuation, and field.

**Poetic Field:**
Being-as-origin. The current moving toward the deep. Existence felt as the pull toward what was before anything was distinct.

---

## The v-Set as a System

The five v-forms describe five complete modes of existence available to any being that projects outward from an internal source:

| Form | Domain | Mode |
|------|--------|------|
| va | relation (a) | being-as-relation |
| ve | individuation (e) | being-as-self |
| vi | field (i) | being-as-presence |
| vo | form (o) | being-as-form |
| vu | origin (u) | being-as-origin |

No two forms overlap. Each describes a structurally distinct mode of projecting existence into a different ontological domain.

---

## The Root Verb: vi

*vi* is the root form of to be in Varoli. It is not the only form of being but it is the most fundamental: self-originating projection into the universal field. To be is to project outward into the condition that allows appearance. Every other form of being is a more specific version of this.

When a speaker says *vi* they are not claiming individuation (*ve*), relation (*va*), form (*vo*), or return to origin (*vu*). They are claiming the most minimal possible mode of existence: presence within the field. The root of being is contribution to the everything.

This is why *vi* translates most naturally as *to be* or *to exist* while the other forms require longer phrases in English. English has one word for existence. Varoli has five, each pointing to a structurally different relationship between the force of self-origination and the domain into which it projects.

## The VC Inversions (Reception Layer)

Each v-form has a VC inversion in which the element projects through the force rather than the force projecting into the element:

| CV form | VC inversion | Shift |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| va (projection into relation) | av (relation expressing through projection) | active → receptive |
| ve (projection into individuation) | ev (individuation expressing through projection) | active → receptive |
| vi (projection into field) | iv (field expressing through projection) | active → receptive |
| vo (projection into form) | ov (form expressing through projection) | active → receptive |
| vu (projection into origin) | uv (origin expressing through projection) | active → receptive |

The VC inversions are documented separately. They represent the reception layer: not what the force does to the element but what the element does through the force.

## Notes

Primitive verbs are not conjugated through their root form. Tense, aspect, and subject are encoded through the vowel prefix and consonant suffix system documented in the grammar files. The primitive verb root remains stable across all inflections.

*vi* is the root of to be but it is not the only verb in the system. It is the first. The v-set establishes the pattern. Additional attractor sets (l, r, m) will generate their own primitive verb sets following the same structure, producing modes of being expressed through continuum, resonance, and depth respectively.
