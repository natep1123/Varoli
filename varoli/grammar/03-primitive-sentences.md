# 03 — Primitive Sentences

This document demonstrates the first valid sentences in Varoli. These sentences use only locked primitives: elemental vowels, attractor consonants, v-set primitive verbs, locked nouns, and subject markers defined in `02-subject-markers.md`.

These sentences prove three things: composability, interpretability, and stability.

---

## Sentence Structure (Minimal)

```
[subject marker] + [verb] + [noun (optional)]
```

Subject marker establishes perspective. Verb establishes mode of being. Noun establishes the domain or object toward which the mode of being is directed.

---

## The Five Primitive Sentences

---

### 1. e vi

**Breakdown:**
- e → self (subject marker)
- vi → self-originating projection into the condition of appearance

**Reading:**
I exist within the field. The minimal statement of being. The root verb alone as a complete sentence.

**Structural note:**
This is the most reduced valid sentence in the language. Subject plus verb. No noun required. The field (*i*) is already encoded in the verb.

---

### 2. e va arOva

**Breakdown:**
- e → self (subject marker)
- va → self-originating projection into relational resonance
- arOva → island (noun, relational medium)

**Reading:**
I exist in relation to the island. Self-originating projection into the relational domain, with the island as the medium being entered.

**Structural note:**
The noun does not receive the verb's action. It names the relational domain the verb is projecting into. The island is not acted upon. It is the medium of relation.

---

### 3. a vi

**Breakdown:**
- a → collective (subject marker)
- vi → self-originating projection into the condition of appearance

**Reading:**
We exist within the field. The collective perspective making the same statement as sentence 1.

**Structural note:**
Same verb, different subject marker. Demonstrates scale shift. The mode of being does not change. The perspective from which it is stated does.

---

### 4. e vo erOva

**Breakdown:**
- e → self (subject marker)
- vo → self-originating projection into stable addressable form
- erOva → person, witness (noun, the form being projected into)

**Reading:**
I exist as a person. Self-originating projection into the domain of stable addressable form, with personhood as the specific form being expressed.

**Structural note:**
*vo* encodes the process of becoming a specific addressable thing. *erOva* names which form. Together they describe existence expressed as embodied personhood.

---

### 5. o ve orro

**Breakdown:**
- o → other (subject marker)
- ve → self-originating projection into individuation
- orro → friend (noun, the individuated identity being expressed)

**Reading:**
You exist as friend. Self-originating projection into individuation, with friendship as the specific identity being maintained.

**Structural note:**
*ve* is required here, not *vi*. Friendship is an identity being claimed, not merely a presence in a field. Individuation (*e*) is the correct domain for identity assignment. Using *vi* would only state that you are present in the field alongside the friend, not that you are the friend.

---

## What These Sentences Prove

**Composability:**
Subject markers, primitive verbs, and nouns combine cleanly without ambiguity. Each element retains its function within the larger structure.

**Interpretability:**
Meaning follows directly from structure. No word requires external definition beyond the locked primitives. The sentences can be read center-outward at every level.

**Stability:**
No rule violations. No function drift. No semantic overlap between sentences. Each sentence describes a structurally distinct mode of being.
