# 02 — Subject Markers

In Varoli, single vowels function as standalone subject markers. They represent the perspective of the statement from which a statement is made.

A subject marker precedes the verb. It does not attach to the verb as a prefix. It stands alone as a separate token.

---

## The Five Subject Markers

| Marker | Perspective | Domain |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| e | self | individuation (ego) |
| a | collective | relational resonance (nos) |
| i | all | field of appearance (omnia) |
| o | other | stable addressable form (tu) |
| u | between | undifferentiated origin (unnamed) |

---

## Rule

**Single vowels may function as standalone subject markers representing conscious perspective.**

The subject marker identifies which perspective is making the statement. It does not modify the verb. The verb retains its full CV structure unchanged.

---

## Examples

```
e vi     → self projects into field (I exist within the field)
a vi     → collective projects into field (we exist within the field)
i vi     → all projects into field (everything exists within the field)
o vi     → other projects into field (you exist within the field)
u vi     → the between-one projects into field
```

Same verb. Different perspectives. The subject marker shifts the perspective without altering the verb's function.

---

## Relationship to the Phonemic System

Subject markers are not grammatical inventions. They are the five elemental vowels used in their most minimal form: a single character standing alone as a declaration of perspective before any force or element is named.

For full definitions of each vowel's ontological domain see `02-vowels.md` in `/varoli/`.

---

## Notes

The subject marker is optional when perspective is clear from context. It is required when perspective is ambiguous or when a statement needs to be explicit about which mode of being is speaking.

*u* as subject marker appears rarely. It marks statements made from the perspective of the dissolving, the between, or the not-yet-returned. Its use is structurally valid but contextually specific.
