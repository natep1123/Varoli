# 04 — Primitive Sentences

A primitive sentence is the smallest complete expression in Varoli: a subject marker, a primitive verb, and a locked noun. It answers three questions simultaneously: from whose perspective, in what mode of existence, and within which domain.

These sentences are not translations. They are structural compositions. Each element is fixed by its function. The sentence is readable from its components without memorization.

This document demonstrates composability, interpretability, and stability across all five primitive verb sets: v (projection), l (continuum), r (resonance), m (depth), and y (collapse). General consonant verb sentences are not included here and will be documented when compound and complex structures are formalized.

---

## The v-Set Sentences

The v-set describes modes of existence through self-originating projection. These five sentences establish the baseline pattern.

---

### Sentence 1: e vi iLi

**Components:**
- e — self (subject marker)
- vi — being-as-presence (v-set verb)
- iLi — the field, the everywhere (locked noun: air palindrome)

**Reading:**
Self is present in the field. The individuated self projecting its existence into the condition of appearance.

**Structural note:**
This is the most reduced possible sentence: first-person perspective, root verb, universal noun. Everything else in the language expands from here.

---

### Sentence 2: a va aVa

**Components:**
- a — collective (subject marker)
- va — being-as-relation (v-set verb)
- aVa — water eternal, the relational medium (locked noun: water palindrome)

**Reading:**
The collective flows as relation within the relational medium. The shared perspective experiencing connection through the element of connection.

**Structural note:**
Subject, verb, and noun share the same element (*a*). This is not redundancy. It is resonance: the relational perspective experiencing relational existence within the relational substrate. The three layers reinforce rather than repeat because each occupies a different grammatical role.

---

### Sentence 3: u vu uMu

**Components:**
- u — unnamed (subject marker)
- vu — being-as-origin (v-set verb)
- uMu — the void, the deep (locked noun: void palindrome)

**Reading:**
The unnamed exists as origin within the void. The between-one projecting existence into the substrate prior to distinction.

**Structural note:**
Like Sentence 2, all three positions share an element. But where Sentence 2 resonates with the warmth of collective relation, Sentence 3 resonates with the gravity of dissolution. The structure is identical. The substrate changes everything.

---

### Sentence 4: i vi aVa

**Components:**
- i — universal (subject marker)
- vi — being-as-presence (v-set verb)
- aVa — water eternal (locked noun)

**Reading:**
The universal field is present within the relational medium. Presence existing within connection.

**Structural note:**
Subject (*i*) and verb (*vi*) share an element, but the noun (*aVa*) introduces a different domain. The field is present, but it is present within water. This demonstrates that the noun is not merely a label for the domain the verb already names — it specifies the substrate the verb operates within. `i vi iLi` and `i vi aVa` contain the same subject and verb but describe structurally different conditions.

---

### Sentence 5: o ve oRo

**Components:**
- o — other (subject marker)
- ve — being-as-self (v-set verb)
- oRo — the mountain, the earth (locked noun: earth palindrome)

**Reading:**
The other exists as a distinct self within the domain of stable form. A bounded form individuating within the element of boundedness.

**Structural note:**
This sentence requires *ve* (being-as-self) rather than *vi* (being-as-presence) because the subject *o* is a specific addressable form. A located, bounded other does not merely exist in the field — it maintains itself as distinct. *vi* would claim only presence. *ve* claims identity. The mountain is not merely present. It is itself.

## The l-Set Sentence

The l-set describes modes of persistence through threading, continuous force. This sentence demonstrates how persistence produces a structurally distinct reading from presence.

---

### Sentence 6: e li iLi

**Components:**
- e — self (subject marker)
- li — persisting-as-presence (l-set verb)
- iLi — the field, the everywhere (locked noun: air palindrome)

**Reading:**
Self persists as presence in the field. The individuated self threading its continuity through the condition of appearance.

**Structural note:**
Compare with Sentence 1 (`e vi iLi`). Both place the self in the field. But `vi` is projection — the self arriving in the field, being there. `li` is continuity — the self maintaining its presence across time, enduring there. The difference is temporal. `e vi iLi` is a snapshot. `e li iLi` is a duration. The sentence structure is identical. The verb changes what kind of existence is described: presence versus persistence. This confirms that the verb alone carries the modal distinction while the sentence pattern remains stable.

---

## The r-Set Sentence

The r-set describes modes of resonance through internally oscillating force. This sentence demonstrates how resonance produces a felt-weight reading distinct from projection.

---

### Sentence 7: o ro oRo

**Components:**
- o — other (subject marker)
- ro — resonating-as-form (r-set verb)
- oRo — the mountain, the earth (locked noun: earth palindrome)

**Reading:**
The other resonates as form within the domain of stable form. A bounded thing vibrating within the element of boundedness.

**Structural note:**
Compare with Sentence 5 (`o ve oRo`). Both place a bounded other within earth. But `ve` is individuation — the other maintaining its distinctness, being itself. `ro` is resonance — the other vibrating, holding weight, being felt. `o ve oRo` is the mountain being the mountain. `o ro oRo` is the mountain humming. The first is identity. The second is frequency. The sentence structure is identical. The verb changes whether the noun is experienced as a self-maintaining form or as a vibrating one. `ro` is the root form of the r-set — resonance meeting its own element — and this sentence demonstrates why: earth resonating within earth is the most natural expression of felt weight in the language.

---

## The m-Set Sentence

The m-set describes modes of descent through inward descending force. This sentence demonstrates how the "descending-into" mode label changes the function of the noun.

---

### Sentence 8: e mu uMu

**Components:**
- e — self (subject marker)
- mu — descending-into-origin (m-set verb)
- uMu — the void, the deep (locked noun: void palindrome)

**Reading:**
Self descends into origin within the void. The individuated self moving inward through the substrate prior to distinction.

**Structural note:**
Compare with Sentence 3 (`u vu uMu`). Both involve the void. But the relationship between verb and noun is different. In Sentence 3, the unnamed *exists as* origin within the void — the noun is the domain of existence. In Sentence 8, the self *descends into* origin within the void — the noun is the domain being entered. The sentence structure is identical. But the verb's directional bias (inward, descending) changes how the noun functions: it becomes a destination rather than a location. The noun is not where the self is. It is where the self is going. This is derivable from *m*'s directional bias and requires no additional grammar. The force carries the directionality. The sentence accommodates it.

This is the structural core of *muku* (to dive): `mu` provides the descent, `ku` provides the hard threshold crossing that completes it.

---

## The y-Set Sentence

The y-set describes modes of becoming through the collapse event. This sentence demonstrates how the event quality of the y-verb changes the temporal register without changing the sentence structure.

---

### Sentence 9: e ye eYe

**Components:**
- e — self (subject marker)
- ye — collapsing-into-individuation (y-set verb)
- eYe — fire, the palindrome of individuation (locked noun: fire palindrome)

**Reading:**
Self collapses into individuation within fire. The individuated self experiencing the moment of becoming distinct.

**Structural note:**
Compare with Sentence 1 (`e vi iLi`). Both use the self as subject. But `vi` is a state — the self present in the field. `ye` is an event — the self crystallizing into distinctness. Sentence 1 has no temporal boundary. Sentence 9 is the lightning strike: a specific moment in which the unresolved becomes resolved. The sentence structure is identical. The temporal register is different. `e vi iLi` could be true for a lifetime. `e ye eYe` is true for an instant.

This sentence also demonstrates triple elemental resonance with fire: self (*e*) experiencing collapse into individuation (*ye*) within fire (*eYe*). Like Sentences 2 and 3, all three positions share an element. But where water resonance is warm and void resonance is gravitational, fire resonance is instantaneous. The element determines the quality of the resonance. The structure holds it.

*eYe* is the only elemental palindrome centered on the operator rather than an attractor consonant. This sentence is the verbal enactment of that structural fact: the self experiencing the collapse event that fire names.

Nine sentences. Five verb sets. Five elemental palindromes. Each sentence proves a different structural property of the system. Together they confirm that the primitive verb inventory composes cleanly with the subject marker and locked noun systems across all five primitive consonant classes.

## Structural Rules

1. A primitive sentence has exactly three elements: subject marker + verb + noun.
2. The subject marker is a single vowel and does not modify the verb.
3. The verb is a CV form from any of the five primitive sets (v, l, r, m, y).
4. The noun is a locked palindromic form (aVa, eYe, iLi, oRo, uMu) or a derived noun.
5. The subject marker, verb, and noun may share elemental substrates. This produces resonance, not redundancy.
6. The verb carries the modal distinction (existence, persistence, resonance, descent, collapse). The sentence structure does not change across verb sets.
7. Directional bias in the verb (e.g., m-set descent) may change the functional relationship between verb and noun without requiring additional grammar.
8. Event verbs (y-set) change the temporal register of the sentence without changing its structure.

---

## Notes

These nine sentences are demonstrations, not an exhaustive inventory. Any combination of subject marker + primitive verb + locked noun is a valid primitive sentence under the generation rules. The nine selected here are chosen because each reveals a structural property that the others do not.

General consonant verb sentences (using forms from `./02-primitive-verbs-extended.md`) will produce valid sentences following the same pattern. Their documentation is deferred until compound and complex sentence structures are formalized.
