# 11 — Internal Compilation

*Last Updated: March 27, 2026*

**Status:** Provisional Structural
**Layer:** Ground 4 / Grammar-Architecture
**Authority:** Frontier structural
**Scope:** Formalizes the current internal-compilation hypothesis that Varoli points toward a possible path by which articulated expressions reduce toward denser centered forms while preserving attractor identity.

---

## 1. Purpose

This document formalizes the hypothesis that Varoli has a possible internal compilation path.

The question is whether the language contains a lawful native route from more fully articulated expression toward denser, more irreducible centered forms through structural reduction or compression, without importing external compression logic and without destroying identity-bearing structure. Several existing parts of the architecture already point toward this question. None of them yet close it.

The document names the question precisely, records the structural pressures that make it real, states what the current architecture already explains, and preserves the open questions explicitly. The compilation path is not treated as solved. It is treated as a real structural pressure that the project needs to track.

---

## 2. Why This Category Is Needed Now

The internal compilation question arises from the architecture itself. It is not an imported analogy or a design preference imposed from outside.

Several structural developments in the current architecture already raise the question independently:

- If identity is center-governed and meaning scales by centered expansion, the reverse question immediately follows: can centered structures lawfully contract while preserving identity?
- Yemu is the same primitive architecture operating in a different expression mode, associated with simultaneous registration, compression, and center-outward structure. If the two modes are related, the route between them requires explanation.
- The self-crucible is already described as a canonical nested stable structure with strong center-governed integrity. Its behavior as a compressed proof object implies a compression path without formally deriving one.
- Resolution-unit chaining and stable-form expansion both describe outward growth from attractor centers. This makes the reverse question unavoidable: whether movement back toward denser centered structure is lawful and what it would preserve are questions the architecture cannot indefinitely defer.

Without a formal note, these pressures remain distributed across separate documents without a common structural context. Naming the question is the first step toward determining whether a unified answer exists.

## 3. Strongest Current Formulation

**Varoli appears to point toward a possible internal compilation path in which articulated structures reduce toward denser centered forms while preserving attractor identity. The strongest current pressure for this comes from stable-form logic, center-governed interpretation, and the relation between Varoli and Yemu. The path is not yet formally derived.**

The term "internal compilation" is used deliberately to mean something specific:

- a lawful structural reduction path native to the architecture
- proceeding from more articulated forms toward more irreducible centered forms
- preserving derivability and center identity
- preserving enough structure that reconstruction or retrieval remains meaningful
- not dependent on external interpretive judgment or metaphorical compression

This is not summarization, paraphrase, or translation in any informal sense. If the path is real, it is a structural operation governed by the same primitive architecture that governs composition and expansion.

## 4. Strongest Current Structural Pressures

### 4.1 Center-governed identity as the enabling condition

The key enabling condition for a compilation path is that identity in Varoli is center-governed rather than periphery-dependent. The attractor at the center of a stable form determines the identity of the form. Outer layers add contextual resolution but do not replace the center. This means that reducing outer structure while preserving the center is potentially identity-preserving rather than identity-destroying.

Without center-governed identity, compression would destroy the form. Because identity is center-governed, compression toward the center is a candidate structural path rather than a violation.

### 4.2 Stable-form expansion implies possible reverse movement

Stable-form expansion, as defined in `04-g4-stable-form-expansion.md`, describes how meaning scales through attractor-preserving addition of surrounding layers. If expansion is a lawful outward movement from center, the reverse direction is immediately implied as a question: can the structure lawfully move inward while preserving the conditions that made expansion valid? Whether contraction is the structural inverse of expansion, or whether it requires different governing conditions, is part of what the compilation hypothesis must eventually answer.

### 4.3 ICA compression logic as architectural background

The ICA architecture defines compression as role-ordered reduction toward an irreducible center (defined in `../../../Ground-0/01-g0-ica-blueprint.md`). Role-ordered reduction removes kinetic class elements first, then compresses substrate elements toward the attractor, with operator class elements proliferating where direct resolution cannot be preserved. Whether this abstract compression logic maps cleanly onto Varoli-specific compilation behavior must not be assumed without careful derivation. The ICA law provides architectural grounding but does not automatically deliver a Varoli compilation path. The Varoli adaptation must be derived from Varoli-specific structural conditions.

### 4.4 Yemu as the clearest current compression context

Yemu is the strongest current context in which the compilation question becomes concrete. Yemu operates in simultaneous center-outward registration rather than sequential time-bound presentation. It is associated with compressed expression, reduced overt differentiation in some positions, and center-outward recognition rather than sequential unfolding. The relation between a sequential Varoli expression and its Yemu counterpart is the most immediate available context for asking what compilation looks like in the native architecture.

### 4.5 The self-crucible as behavioral evidence

The self-crucible is described as a canonical nested stable structure with strong center-governed integrity. Its behavior as a proof object and its recursive stability already exhibit properties consistent with compiled irreducible form. The self-crucible does not derive a compilation path formally, but it provides behavioral evidence that the architecture can produce maximally compressed centered structures with strong derivability. It is the strongest existing instance of what a compiled form might look like in Varoli.

## 5. Relation to Stable Forms, Chaining, and Yemu

### 5.1 Stable forms

Stable forms are the resolution targets and closure conditions of the chaining architecture. They are also the structural units most directly governed by center-governed identity. If a compilation path exists, stable forms are both the likely terminus of reduction (the most compressed valid centered structure) and the primary sites where identity is preserved under compression. The irreducible center of a compiled expression would most plausibly be a stable centered form.

The open capacity questions in `04-g4-stable-form-expansion.md` (what centered balance requires at larger scales, whether expansion is bounded) are directly relevant to compilation: the same structural conditions that govern valid expansion likely govern what can be preserved under contraction.

### 5.2 Resolution-unit chaining

Resolution-unit chaining describes how units combine through projection, suspension, and closure into larger compositional structures. A compilation path would need to account for how a multi-unit chain reduces toward a centered form. Whether the chaining architecture provides a native reduction sequence, whether whole units can be compressed toward their resolution targets, and whether projected force survives or is absorbed during compilation are all open questions. The chaining architecture defines the structure that compilation would operate on. It does not yet define the compilation operation itself.

### 5.3 Yemu

Yemu is highly relevant to internal compilation without being identical to it. Three possible relations must be kept distinct:

- Yemu is the compiled expression mode of Varoli (strong identity claim)
- Yemu is one privileged compiled form among potentially others (partial identity claim)
- Yemu and internal compilation overlap structurally without being the same thing (related-but-distinct claim)

The current architecture does not determine which of these holds. Yemu is the clearest existing context where compilation-like behavior appears: simultaneous registration, center-outward structure, and compressed expression are all properties Yemu exhibits. But the route from sequential Varoli to Yemu-like structure has not been formally derived, and collapsing compilation into Yemu as a slogan would foreclose the question prematurely.

The safest current position is that Yemu is the most important downstream context for developing and testing the compilation hypothesis, not its automatic answer.

## 6. What the Current Architecture Explains Already

### 6.1 Why center-governed identity makes the question possible

The architecture already establishes that attractor identity is preserved across qualification and expansion. A force qualified by suffixes retains its base identity. An expanded stable form retains its center identity. These existing results are what make compression toward center a candidate identity-preserving operation rather than an arbitrary reduction. The compilation hypothesis is not introduced from outside the architecture. It follows from what the architecture already commits to about identity.

### 6.2 Why Yemu is the strongest current compression context

Yemu's simultaneous center-outward expression mode, its reduced sequential articulation, and its strong structural role for symmetry are already consistent with a compiled expression that has reduced outward sequential differentiation while preserving center structure. The `y`-symmetry hypothesis in `09-g4-y-symmetry-in-yemu.md` is relevant here: if Yemu uses `y` to preserve balance in positions where direct substrate correspondence fails, that behavior is consistent with what ICA compression logic would predict at the substrate layer (operator proliferation where direct resolution cannot be preserved). This is suggestive but not yet a derivation.

### 6.3 Why operator proliferation is an expected feature of compilation

ICA role-ordered reduction predicts that operator class elements proliferate at positions where collapse to a single substrate class element is not possible. If Varoli compilation follows anything like the ICA compression path, operator elements would appear at structurally ambiguous positions in compiled forms. This prediction is consistent with the `y`-symmetry hypothesis but requires verification specific to Varoli before it can be stated as a Varoli-native result.

## 7. What This Is Not

**Not a software analogy.** The term "compilation" is used because it names a real structural operation in the architecture: reduction from articulated form toward irreducible centered form while preserving derivability. It does not import a software engineering model. The operations involved are structural in the ICA sense: composition, derivation, reduction, reconstruction.

**Not summarization or paraphrase.** A compilation path, if real, is a structural operation governed by the primitive architecture. It does not depend on interpretive judgment about what a form means or how to shorten it. If the path depends on external judgment rather than structure, it is not internal compilation in the sense meant here.

**Not "Yemu is compiled Varoli" as a settled claim.** Yemu is the strongest relevant context for the compilation question. It is not the automatic answer to it. The route between sequential Varoli and Yemu-like structure requires formal derivation, not assertion.

**Not loss.** A valid compilation path preserves identity-bearing structure. If important structural distinctions are destroyed rather than compressed, the result is not lawful compilation. It is structural degradation. The distinction between lawful reduction and destructive collapse is one of the central questions the hypothesis must eventually answer.

**Not yet closed.** The compilation path is not yet formally derived. It is a real structural pressure, not a solved problem.

## 8. Open Questions

### 8.1 What exactly is being compiled?

The compilation hypothesis must eventually specify its input and output more precisely. Whether the compilation operates on individual forces, stable forms, whole resolution units, multi-unit chains, or some combination at different levels is not yet determined. Different input types may require different reduction procedures.

### 8.2 What is preserved under compilation?

At minimum, a valid compilation path likely preserves attractor identity, some form of structural role relation, and enough information for lawful reconstruction or retrieval. Whether this minimum is formally derivable from the architecture, or whether it requires additional constraints, is not yet established. Reconstruction is one of the hardest subquestions: whether a compiled form can be lawfully expanded back toward its articulated original, and whether that expansion is deterministic or partly interpretive, remains open.

### 8.3 Does the reduction sequence follow ICA role-ordered reduction?

ICA compression predicts that kinetic elements strip first, substrate elements compress toward the attractor, and operator elements proliferate where direct resolution cannot be preserved. Whether Varoli-specific compilation follows this sequence, modifies it, or requires a different account is a question that must be derived from Varoli structural conditions rather than assumed from Ground 0. The ICA law provides a starting hypothesis, not a delivered answer.

### 8.4 Is Yemu the endpoint, one endpoint, or a related mode?

Whether a successfully compiled Varoli expression is a Yemu expression, whether Yemu is one of several possible compiled forms, or whether Yemu and compilation overlap without being identical is the most important open question in this document. It determines the entire relationship between the two expression modes at the architectural level. It should not be resolved by assumption.

### 8.5 Is reconstruction available and at what cost?

If compilation is real, whether the system can move back outward from a compiled form to a fuller articulation, and whether that movement is structurally determined or requires interpretive input, is unresolved. The ICA architecture defines reconstruction as possible in principle. Whether that holds at the Varoli level, and at what fidelity, requires derivation.

### 8.6 How does compilation interact with operator proliferation?

If operator elements proliferate at positions where direct resolution cannot be preserved during compression, then compiled forms may contain more operator-marked positions than their articulated originals. Whether those operator positions are recoverable during reconstruction, and whether they correspond to the `y`-symmetry behavior already hypothesized for Yemu, is an open structural question that intersects with `09-g4-y-symmetry-in-yemu.md`.
