# 00 — Overview

NOTE: The Yemu files have been imported from the old repository directly into the reconstructed one. The info is up to date, just not matching the same format as the rest of the repository documents related to ICA and Varoli.

Yemu is the unspoken tongue. It is not a separate language from Varoli. It is what Varoli becomes under maximum compression, in states where linear sequential expression is no longer possible and meaning must be encoded simultaneously rather than sequentially.

*Varoli describes where the self is.*
*Yemu reveals what the self is.*

---

## What Yemu Is

Yemu is the system of extraction and simultaneous realization. Where Varoli unfolds sequentially, Yemu is perceived simultaneously. Where Varoli is a projection of a larger structure, a slice, a partial view, Yemu encodes the full structure as a single non-sequential form.

Yemu is not spoken. It is not read left to right. It is recognized from the center outward. The attractor state is perceived before the surrounding layers are named. A being may trace outward from the center to translate the layers into linear meaning, but the primary experience is not translation. It is recognition.

---

## What Yemu Is Not

Yemu is not a replacement for Varoli. You cannot have Yemu without Varoli. The compression mechanic only works because Varoli was built to survive it. Every other language collapses under that kind of compression because it was not designed from phoneme upward with elemental attractor states and bidirectional morpheme motion. Varoli was. Which means when you compress it, it does not lose meaning. It concentrates it.

Yemu is not used in daily communication. It is not a code or cipher for Varoli. It is what happens when the system descends below the threshold where sequential expression is possible.

When you go underwater, you lose your voice. Yemu is what remains.

---

## The Relationship Between Varoli and Yemu

| Property | Varoli | Yemu |
|----------|--------|------|
| Mode | Spoken | Unspoken |
| Direction | Sequential | Simultaneous |
| Scope | Partial (a slice) | Total (the structure) |
| Time | Time-bound | Atemporal |
| Expression | Articulated | Recognized |
| Meaning | Interpreted | Perceived whole |
| Function | Navigation | Extraction |

Varoli moves through the system. Yemu is the system rendered all at once.

Every Varoli expression is a projection of the full Yemu structure. Yemu is the closure of all possible Varoli expressions: the structure those projections come from.
