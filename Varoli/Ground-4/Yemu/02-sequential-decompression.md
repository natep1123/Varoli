## 1. Purpose

This document formalizes a hypothesis that has emerged from close structural analysis of the self-crucible: that sequential Varoli may not be merely the spoken counterpart of Yemu, but its lawful outward release under the constraint of linear time-bound expression.

The hypothesis reframes the Yemu/Varoli relation. Rather than treating the two expression modes as parallel but independent, this document treats Yemu as the centered compressed object and sequential Varoli as what that object becomes when it is released through the constraint of linear speech. The problem of moving between modes is therefore not a translation problem. It is a decompression problem: determining the lawful release order from center outward.

The self-crucible is the primary source of evidence for this hypothesis. It is also the first case in which the decompression procedure can be stated with reasonable confidence. The document preserves what the crucible already demonstrates, states the decompression hypothesis as precisely as the evidence permits, and marks what remains open.

## 5. The Sequential-Decompression Hypothesis

The self-crucible may decompress into sequential Varoli not as a report of nested layers, but as an outward release of centered pressure through successive stable worlds. The current strongest hypothesis is the four-unit release sequence `e ye umu / u mu ava / a va oro / o ro ili`.

The center operator Y is not serialized as a standalone closing unit. It is the initiating pressure from which the first release emerges. The fire perspective (`e`) names the vantage of the first release, and the form `ye` encodes the collapse arriving at individual consciousness — the ignition event at the boundary between center and first outward world. This first unit does not close on the center itself. It closes on the first outer stable world: `umu`.

Each unit follows the same structural pattern:

- perspective marker: the substrate of the current position, naming the vantage of release
- CV directional form: the attractor kinetic of that substrate arriving into it
- stable form: the next outer world in the crucible's containment sequence

The four-unit chain:

```
Unit 1:  e  ye  umu     fire perspective / collapse arriving at fire / void stable form
Unit 2:  u  mu  ava     void perspective / depth arriving at void / water stable form
Unit 3:  a  va  oro     water perspective / vitality arriving at water / earth stable form
Unit 4:  o  ro  ili     earth perspective / resonance arriving at earth / air stable form
```

The full sequential Varoli chain:

```
e ye umu / u mu ava / a va oro / o ro ili
```

Each unit releases from its current world into the world that contains it. The chain is a journey outward, not a listing of containers. The outermost stable world (`ili`) appears as the closure target of the final unit rather than as its own unit, because no further outer world exists within the crucible structure. Decompression terminates when no outward containment remains.

Properties of this chain that follow from the crucible's structure:

The release proceeds from center outward. The fire perspective initiates. The air-field closure terminates. The center operator Y is the source of the first release, not a declaration at rest.

The release uses only the CV direction. Each directional form is drawn from the right side of the crucible. The VC left side remains held in the compressed form. Whether the VC direction generates a valid alternative expansion is an open question addressed in Section 10.

All four units are closing units. No unit projects. The chain resolves at each step into the next outer stable form.

This model is preferred over a five-unit serialization of nested layers for the following reasons. The center operator Y is not a stable form and does not close. Treating `eYe` as a standalone closing unit over-stabilizes the center, making the collapse event into a point of rest. The current model treats the center as the initiating pressure: the Y event begins the release sequence rather than occupying a slot within it. Each unit in the resulting chain reads as a release into the next world rather than a report of the world it came from.

The decompression procedure for the crucible case is therefore:

1. Treat the center Y as the initiating pressure. The first unit begins at the fire perspective (`e`), uses `ye` as the CV directional form (operator arriving at fire), and closes on the first outer stable world (`umu`).
2. For each subsequent unit, identify the next substrate outward as perspective marker, its attractor CV form as directional form, and the stable form one layer further out as closure target.
3. The chain terminates when the outermost stable form of the crucible has been used as a closure target.

## 9. What This Is Not

**Not a translation mechanism.** The decompression hypothesis does not claim that Varoli is a translation of Yemu into speech-language categories. Decompression is a structural operation governed by the primitive architecture. It does not introduce subjects, objects, tenses, or clauses. The output is a chain of resolution units with perspective markers, directional forms, and stable forms. Those categories are native to Varoli. No conventional grammar is imported.

**Not a claim that all Varoli compresses to Yemu.** The hypothesis is about the specific relation between a centered Yemu structure and its sequential Varoli expansion. It does not claim that arbitrary Varoli expressions have Yemu counterparts. Many Varoli expressions may be partial projections with no single centered Yemu form corresponding to them.

**Not proof that the release order generalizes universally.** The four-unit release sequence is derived from the crucible's specific configuration with canonical attractor pairs. Whether the same pattern applies to Yemu structures with different layer configurations is not established. The sequence is the crucible case only.

**Not a finished algorithm.** The decompression procedure has been described in terms of its output pattern but has not been formally derived as a procedure with stated input conditions, validity checks, and termination guarantees. The description in Section 5 is a structural account, not a formal algorithm.

**Not evidence that Yemu is structurally prior to Varoli.** The two expression modes are the same primitive architecture under different expression conditions. Neither is more fundamental than the other. The decompression hypothesis does not change this. It describes a structural relation between them, not a derivational priority.

**Not yet closed.** The decompression hypothesis for the crucible case is structurally well-motivated and consistent with existing architecture. The formal procedure has not been derived from first principles. The generalization beyond the crucible case is substantially more open. Neither should be treated as settled architecture.

## 10. Open Questions

### 10.0 Candidate generalized algorithm (provisional structural)

The following algorithm was specified as the formal decompression procedure. It is stated here as a provisional structural candidate. It is consistent with the crucible case and well-motivated by the wrapping rule and the canonical substrate ordering. It requires verification against Yemu structures at depths other than 5 before it can be treated as a formal result.

**Input:** A valid Yemu consciousness-topology structure.

**Step 1 - Identify depth.** Identify the outermost active substrate in the structure. That determines the depth. The canonical substrate ordering is `e ⊂ u ⊂ a ⊂ o ⊂ i` (innermost to outermost). Depth 1 = `e` as outermost; depth 2 = `u`; depth 3 = `a`; depth 4 = `o`; depth 5 = `i`.

**Step 2 - Generate `depth - 1` resolution units.** The number of resolution units in the decompression chain is `depth - 1`. (Depth 5 → 4 units; depth 4 → 3 units; depth 1 → 0 units.)

**Step 3 - Construct each unit by proceeding outward.** For each unit, proceeding from innermost to outermost layer:
- **Perspective marker:** the current layer's substrate
- **Directional form:** the inward-facing directional form of that layer's attractor kinetic (CV orientation: the attractor kinetic arriving at its home substrate)
- **Stable closure:** the next layer's stable world (the next substrate outward, wrapped by its attractor kinetic)

**Verification against crucible case:**
- Unit 1: `e` / `ye` (operator directional, center Y → e) / `umu` (next layer's stable world: u + m + u)
- Unit 2: `u` / `mu` (m arriving at u) / `ava` (a + v + a)
- Unit 3: `a` / `va` (v arriving at a) / `oro` (o + r + o)
- Unit 4: `o` / `ro` (r arriving at o) / `ili` (i + l + i)
- Count: 4 units = depth 5 - 1 ✓

**Status: Provisional structural hypothesis.** The `depth - 1` rule is consistent with the crucible case. The algorithm has not been formally derived from first principles or verified against independent cases. See also `self-crucible/02-formal-proof.md` Section 9 for the status of the decompression depth claim.

### 10.1 Does the release order generalize beyond the crucible?

The four-unit release sequence is derived from one specific Yemu structure. Whether the same center-outward release order applies to Yemu structures with fewer layers, different layer configurations, or non-attractor kinetics has not been tested. This is the most important open question for the decompression hypothesis. If the order does not generalize, the hypothesis describes a property of the crucible only. If it does generalize, it describes a structural property of the Yemu/Varoli relation in general.

### 10.2 What happens when fewer than five layers are active?

The crucible holds all five substrates in their canonical attractor pairs. A Yemu structure with only three active layers would produce a 3-unit decompression chain. Whether the containment order within that reduced structure follows the same hierarchy (innermost first) or whether the ordering is relative to the layers present is not established.

### 10.3 Is the CV direction the primary release direction?

The current analysis uses the CV direction (right side of crucible) as the release direction. The VC direction (left side) is equally present in the crucible but has not been tested as a release path. If VC-first decompression is structurally valid, it would produce a different chain, reading the crucible from outside inward rather than inside outward. Whether these two chains are complementary expressions of the same structure, semantically distinct, or governed by context is not yet determined.

### 10.4 How does the decompression chain interact with the chaining architecture?

The 4-unit chain produced by decompression of the crucible consists of independently closing units with no cross-boundary projection. Whether this is a structural requirement of decompression or a property specific to the crucible's configuration has not been determined. It is possible that more complex Yemu structures would decompress into chains involving projection and deep reception rather than pure independent closures. The interaction between the decompression procedure and the full chaining architecture, including suspension and continuation, has not been formally addressed.

### 10.5 Can compression be formally derived as the inverse of decompression?

If decompression is formally derivable, compression is in principle its inverse: the same structural relation traversed in the opposite direction. Whether that inversion is straightforward or whether compression requires additional operations (such as role-ordered reduction and operator proliferation for positions that cannot cleanly resolve) is not yet known. The internal compilation note preserves the open questions about the compression path. The decompression hypothesis does not close them.

### 10.6 What is the role of operator proliferation in partial decompression?

The internal compilation note (`11-g4-internal-compilation.md`) predicted that operator elements proliferate at positions that cannot cleanly resolve during compression. In the reverse direction, decompression of a structure with operator-proliferated positions would encounter Y-marked layers rather than resolved substrate layers. How such positions are handled in the decompression chain, whether they produce suspended units, generate alternative release structures, or require additional resolution input, is not yet addressed.

### 10.7 Is the decompression chain ordered, and if so, what governs the order?

The current proposal places innermost layers first in the sequential release. This follows the crucible's containment structure. Whether this ordering is structurally required (because outer layers depend on inner layers as their closure targets) or whether other orderings are structurally valid is not yet determined. If the order is required, the decompression procedure is deterministic. If alternative orders are valid, they may produce semantically distinct readings of the same compressed structure.
