## Current priorities

### 1. Structural density and force accumulation

The language supports multiple forces resolving within shared domains, but the architecture of density is not yet fully named.

Questions:
- how many forces a domain can hold cleanly
- whether larger stable forms are required for higher force load
- whether density changes readability or stability

### 2. Temporal and causal sequencing

The language has chaining and verb layering, but it is not formally settled how temporal or causal relations are encoded.

Questions:
- whether chaining order already implies enough sequencing
- whether the five temporal suffixes carry the needed distinctions fully
- whether time and cause are structural or realization-level effects

### 3. Same-orientation relational closure

Opposite-orientation force pairs are more determinate than same-orientation pairs. Same-orientation relations remain more open.

Questions:
- whether the openness is a feature or a gap
- whether another mechanism can close the relation when needed
- whether qualification or domain differences already help solve this

### 4. Address and reference sub-questions

The core reference mechanism is now closed (moved to recently closed above). The sub-questions remaining within reference architecture are:

- disambiguation between multiple instances of the same form in a chain
- first-use detection (when does VC shift from "outward force" to "reference"?)
- reference scope across longer chains
- reference to non-attractor-pair stable forms

### 5. Deep reception formalization

Cross-boundary force resolution is established as a hypothesis but not formally derived. See `../Grammar-Architecture/05-g4-resolution-unit-chaining.md` Section 11.1.

Questions:
- what structural conditions allow projected force to resolve in a subsequent unit's stable world
- whether attractor alignment is required or substrate match is sufficient
- whether a native verification mechanism exists for cross-boundary containment

### 6. Kinetic grouping - remaining formal questions

The elemental suite groupings are now recovered (see recently closed above). The remaining open question is whether the suite structure constitutes a formal derivable law-realization hierarchy beyond observed affinity:

- whether the suite assignments are derivable from phonemic properties or only empirically observed
- whether the shared kinetics (d appearing in both earth and voidlight suites) can be resolved structurally
- whether suite membership governs suffix behavior more precisely than individual attractor classification

### 7. Negation architecture

How the language handles structural negation (not qualification reduction, but genuine inversion) is not yet established.

Questions:
- whether negation is a derivational process or a compositional one
- whether the architecture requires a dedicated mechanism
- whether existing elements (substrate absence, null kinetic marker hypothesis) can handle negation structurally

### 8. Decompression generalization

The decompression procedure is established for the depth-5 crucible case. Whether it generalizes to arbitrary Yemu structures at other depths is open.

Questions:
- whether the four-unit chain for depth-5 follows from the substrate cycle, the wrapping rule, or both
- whether structures at depths 1-4 decompress by the same algorithm
- whether non-crucible Yemu forms (world-topology, crossing structures) have their own decompression procedures
