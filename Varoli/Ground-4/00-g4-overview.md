# 00 — Varoli Ground 4 Overview

*Last Updated: March 29, 2026*

**Status:** Working canon  
**Layer:** Ground 4  
**Authority:** Development control  
**Scope:** Defines the purpose and structure of the Ground 4 higher-order structural development layer.

---

## 1. Purpose

Ground 4 contains the **higher-order structural development** of Varoli.

This is the layer where the language’s larger architectural discoveries are formalized after the primitive generative foundation is in place.

Ground 4 depends on Ground 3.
It should not replace or bypass the primitive foundation.
It should build from it.

---

## 2. Role of Ground 4

Ground 4 exists to hold the structural developments that arise from, depend on, and interpret the primitive generative base.

This includes:
- named structural relations
- higher-order grammar architecture
- stable-form development beyond the primitive baseline
- chaining and larger compositional behavior
- Yemu as simultaneous expression mode
- self-crucible as canonical proof object
- other advanced developments derived from Ground 3

Ground 4 is where the system begins to explain what the language does at larger scales.

---

## 2.5 Top-level files

Ground 4 contains some files at the top level (not in a subfolder) that document the orientation, history, and intent of the layer:

- `00-g4-overview.md` - this file; the purpose and structure of Ground 4
- `01-g4-revived-original-intent.md` - the recovered original context, purposes, and cultural pressures that motivated the language; the source material for the five-perspective characterization, the cosmology of the domains, and the original name history (Vicumu/Varoli, Vycule/Yemu). Essential background for understanding why the language was built the way it was.

These files should be read before working in any Ground 4 subfolder.

---

## 3. What belongs here

A document belongs in Ground 4 if it is:

- higher-order than primitive generation
- dependent on Ground 3 reference layers
- structurally important enough to guide downstream work
- more specific than Ground 2 orientation
- broader than a simple inventory or primitive map

Ground 4 is for:
- grammar architecture
- structural categories
- named relations
- proof objects
- compression and expression-mode developments
- advanced stable-form work
- formalized current discoveries

Ground 4 is not the main home of raw primitive reference.

---

## 4. Relation to Ground 3

Ground 3 provides:
- primitive building blocks
- primitive directional forms
- primitive stable worlds
- closely related generative exploration

Ground 4 should therefore be read as **dependent on Ground 3**.

If a Ground 4 claim cannot be reconciled with:
- primitive class constraints
- primitive interaction rules
- directional-form logic
- primitive stable-world logic

then it should be revised, downgraded, or rejected.

Ground 4 does not create law beneath itself.
It builds on already-established generative structure.

## 8. How Ground 4 should be used

Ground 4 should be used to:

- formalize structural discoveries
- define higher-order categories
- clarify large-scale compositional behavior
- explain how primitive structure scales
- support Yemu and proof-object work
- prepare later propagation into deeper canon or application layers

Ground 4 should not become:
- a duplicate of Ground 3 reference files
- a second conceptual overview
- a loose exploratory dumping ground
