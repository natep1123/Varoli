## Reading Principle

A primitive directional form is a two-primitive composition in which a kinetic force and a substrate domain are arranged in one of two orientations.

**CV (force → domain): arrival.** The kinetic enters the substrate. Force arriving into a domain. Inward-facing. Compression toward center.

**VC (domain → force): departure.** The substrate expresses through the kinetic. A domain projecting outward through a force. Outward-facing. Expansion away from center.

These two orientations are structurally distinct. Reversing the order changes the relation itself. CV and VC are not variant spellings. They encode different directional realities.

Directional forms are transitional. They lean. They do not close. They do not resolve around an attractor. In grammar, these are the primitive verbs.

---

## Structural Categories

The 220 forms divide into three structural categories based on the kinetic's class membership.

**Attractor directional forms (40).** Kinetic is an attractor consonant (v, l, r, m). These carry elemental affinity. Each set has a root form where the force meets its own element. The attractor sets describe modes of existence: being, persisting, resonating, descending.

**Operator directional forms (10).** Kinetic position is occupied by the operator (y). The y-set is structurally distinct: it describes modes of becoming (collapse events) rather than modes of existence. The CV/VC distinction maps onto collapse/dissolution rather than active/receptive.

**General directional forms (170).** Kinetic is a general consonant. These carry no elemental affinity. All five domain placements are equally fundamental. No form in these sets is a root form.

---

## Directionality Encoding

The CV/VC distinction encodes structural directionality beyond grammatical voice:

- CV faces inward: arrival, entry, descent, compression toward center
- VC faces outward: departure, exit, ascent, expansion away from center

This is operative throughout the Yemu diving system. Zone declarations are palindromic because they contain both directions simultaneously. Gate codes place VC (departure) on the left and CV (arrival) on the right, with the operator between them as the collapse event separating release from crystallization.

Every directional form carries a built-in orientation. The CV form faces inward. The VC form faces outward. The palindromic stable form holds both.

# Operator Set

The operator is neither substrate nor kinetic. The y-set is structurally distinct from all consonant sets. The attractor verbs describe modes of existence. The y-verbs describe modes of becoming: the moment an unresolved state collapses into a specific domain.

The CV/VC distinction for the y-set does not map onto active/receptive as it does for consonant sets. It maps onto collapse/dissolution: CV describes the moment a domain crystallizes from superposition. VC describes the moment a domain releases back into superposition. This asymmetry is derivable from the operator's unique structural position: `y` is not a force with a direction but an event with two phases.

---

# General Consonant Sets

These seventeen consonants carry no elemental affinity. They are applied forces that move between elements, across them, and through them. Their verb sets describe modes of interaction rather than modes of existence native to a specific substrate. All five domain placements in each set are equally fundamental. No general set has a root form.

General consonant VC inversions describe a domain expressing itself as the quality of motion the consonant names. Where attractor VC forms produce "the domain expressing through its native force," general VC forms produce "the domain manifesting as this applied force."

Twenty-two kinetic/operator sets. Five domains. Two orientations. Two hundred twenty primitive directional forms. No overlap.

**CV (110 forms):** Force arriving into domain. Inward-facing. These are the active verbs: modes of existence, persistence, resonance, descent, becoming, crossing, compressing, emerging, contacting, resisting, seeking, recognizing, containing, binding, oscillating, dissolving, gathering, releasing, abrading, catalyzing, and dampening within each of five domains.

**VC (110 forms):** Domain expressing through force. Outward-facing. These are the receptive verbs: each domain manifesting as the quality of motion the force names. The domain does not passively receive. It actively expresses itself through the force.

**The y-set exception:** For the operator, CV/VC maps onto collapse/dissolution rather than active/receptive. CV y-forms describe the moment a domain crystallizes. VC y-forms describe the moment a domain releases back into superposition.

## What This Inventory Does Not Include

**Verb layering.** Layered forms (CV+C, CV+CC) are documented in the verb layering grammar document. They are compositional extensions of these primitive directional forms, not separate inventory items.

**Compound verbs.** Multi-consonant verb compositions beyond suffix layering are not yet formalized.

**Sentence-level compositions.** How these directional forms compose with perspective markers and stable forms into resolution units is documented in the grammar files.

This inventory covers the complete primitive directional form space: every valid two-primitive composition of kinetic/operator with substrate, in both orientations.
