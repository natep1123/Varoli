# 00 - Same-Envelope Stable Worlds Expansion

*Last Updated: March 28, 2026*

**Status:** Working lexicon expansion. Structurally derived from canon primitives.
**Scope:** All 110 same-envelope stable forms (VCV where both vowels match). 22 centers (4 attractor, 1 operator, 17 general) across 5 substrate domains.
**Authority:** Subordinate to `../Primitive-Building-Blocks/00-primitive-building-blocks-expanded.md` for primitive definitions. Subordinate to current Ground 2 terminology for structural categories.

---

## Reading Principle

A same-envelope stable form is a three-part centered structure: substrate, center, same substrate repeated. The center is the attractor. The surrounding vowel is the domain.

Read each form in this order:

1. Identify the center. This is the attractor: the force or event held in balance.
2. Identify the envelope. This is the domain: the substrate condition within which the center is held.
3. Derive the stable form: the thing that exists when this center is stabilized within this domain.

The center is typographically marked with capitalization. The form reads from center outward. These are stable forms, not directional forms. They do not lean. They do not arrive. They are held.

A directional form (CV) describes force arriving into a domain. A stable form (VCV) describes what exists when that force is held in centered balance within that domain. These are structurally different. The readings below describe stable worlds, not arrivals.

---

## Structural Categories

The 110 forms divide into three structural categories based on the center's class membership.

**Attractor stable forms (20).** Center is an attractor consonant (v, l, r, m). These carry elemental affinity. Four are native pairings where the attractor meets its own element (aVa, iLi, oRo, uMu). Sixteen are cross-domain forms.

**Operator stable forms (5).** Center is the operator (y). One is the native pairing where the operator meets fire (eYe). Four are cross-domain forms.

**General stable forms (85).** Center is a general consonant. These carry no elemental affinity. All five domain placements are equally fundamental. No form in this set is a root form.

## Notation

For single-character centers, capitalization is straightforward: `aVa`, `oRo`.

For digraph centers (th, ch, sh), this document capitalizes the full digraph: `aTHa`, `aCHa`, `aSHa`. This convention is provisional and subject to formal typographic rules when those are closed.

## What This Inventory Does Not Include

**Cross-envelope stable forms** (V1-C-V2 where the vowels differ). 440 asymmetric VCV forms exist. Their grammatical role is undefined. They are excluded from this inventory.

**Five-character stable forms** (V1-C1-C2-C1-V1). The pattern proven by `arOva` extends the stable form space to 2,310 additional forms. Those are documented separately.

**Doubled-center forms** (V1-CC-V1). Excluded from active use until doubled consonant semantics are structurally defined.

This inventory covers the complete same-envelope three-character stable form space. It is the first layer of the lexicon.
