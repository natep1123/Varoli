## 1. Structural category

An **operator directional form** is a directional form in which the operator `y` occupies the directional position rather than a kinetic consonant.

This is not a violation of non-overlap. `y` does not become kinetic when it occupies the directional position. It contributes its invariant function - context-resolved collapse through superposition - to the composition. The structural outcome is a directional form whose nature is resolution of superposition into a substrate, rather than kinetic force arriving at or expressing through a substrate.

Operator directional forms are therefore structurally distinct from kinetic-substrate directional forms in role-class contribution: the kinetic-substrate form encodes force arriving or departing; the operator directional form encodes the collapse event arriving or the substrate expressing through the collapse mechanism.

**Derivation basis:** Law 7 (scale realization without function mutation). y's invariant function - governing the transition from unresolved to resolved state - manifests at the directional-form level as a collapse event in relation to a specific substrate domain. The function does not change. The scope of its application is a single substrate domain rather than a whole structure.

**Supporting authority:** The operator schema (`03-g3-operator.md` Section 4.2) already records that y in consonant position "marks the active event of collapse within structure." The primitive interaction rules document (`05-g3-primitive-interaction-rules.md` Section 7) licenses y in directional forms. The sequential decompression document (`../Ground-4/Yemu/02-sequential-decompression.md` Section 11.7) identified the absent formal category and marked it as the primary unresolved structural item. This document provides that formal category.

## 2. The 10 operator directional forms

Both CV and VC directions are maintained. They encode structurally distinct realities, consistent with binary directionality (Law 1).

**CV forms:** collapse arriving at the substrate - the operator acting toward the domain, the resolution event directed inward.

**VC forms:** the substrate expressing through collapse - the domain undergoing the resolution process, the collapse mechanism expressing outward from the domain.

## 3. The CV/VC distinction

CV and VC operator directional forms are not interchangeable. As with all directional forms, orientation carries structural information (Law 1).

- **CV** (ya, ye, yi, yo, yu): the collapse event moves toward the substrate. The substrate is what resolution arrives at. The form describes what happens to the domain when the collapse event reaches it.
- **VC** (ay, ey, iy, oy, uy): the substrate moves through collapse. The collapse mechanism is what the domain expresses through. The form describes what the domain does as it undergoes its own resolution event.

These are two different structural realities for each attractor pair. `ye` (collapse arriving at individuation) and `ey` (individuation expressing through collapse) describe different events. Do not flatten them.

`y` in operator directional forms is still operator class. It does not become kinetic by occupying the directional position. Class identity is invariant.
