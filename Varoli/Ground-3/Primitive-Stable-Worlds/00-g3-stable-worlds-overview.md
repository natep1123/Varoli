# 00 — Varoli G3 Stable Worlds Overview

*Last Updated: March 28, 2026*

**Status:** Working reference  
**Layer:** Ground 3 / Primitive-Stable-Worlds  
**Authority:** Build-layer overview  
**Scope:** Defines the role, structure, and organization of primitive stable worlds in Varoli.

---

## 1. Purpose

This folder contains the primitive stable worlds of Varoli.

A primitive stable world is the smallest centered palindromic world-unit that can hold stable structure.

This folder exists to make the stable-world layer:
- searchable
- learnable
- generative
- easy to validate
- easy to compare before higher stable-form work begins

---

## 2. Core structural idea

A stable world requires:
- a valid center
- balanced structure around that center
- odd character count

This means stable palindromic structure-groups begin at:

- 3
- 5
- 7
- 9
- and so on

The smallest full stable world is therefore a **3-character** form.

This is the first complete centered stable structure available to the language.

---

## 3. Why 3 is the first stable world

A single primitive may still be meaningful, but it is not yet a full centered stable world.

A 3-character form is the first structure that can provide:

- a center
- a left side
- a right side
- readable balance
- stable identity

At the primitive level, this gives the pattern:

### VCV
**Formula:** substrate + center + substrate

At this stage of the build, the strongest primitive stable-world baseline is the **same-envelope** form, where the left and right substrates match.

Examples:
- `aVa`
- `eYe`
- `iLi`
- `oRo`
- `uMu`

---

## 4. Primitive stable-world categories in this folder

### Same-envelope stable worlds
These are the primary primitive baseline.

Their structure is:

- same outer substrate
- centered kinetic or operator
- 3-character stable world

Examples:
- `aVa`
- `aSa`
- `aLa`
- `aDa`
- `aFa`

These are the cleanest first map of the primitive stable-world field.

### Cross-envelope stable worlds
These are structurally important, but remain a more exploratory map for now.

Their structure is still primitive `VCV`, but the left and right substrates differ.

Examples:
- `aVu`
- `eVo`
- `iRa`

These are held separately so the same-envelope baseline remains clean.

---

## 6. Current build rule

At this stage:

- same-envelope worlds are the primary stable-world baseline
- cross-envelope worlds are preserved as an exploratory next step
- expanded stable worlds do not belong in this folder yet
- CVC force-closure candidates do not belong in this folder yet

---

## 7. Use rule

When reading a primitive stable world:

1. identify the outer substrate envelope
2. identify the center
3. derive the center’s invariant function
4. derive what it means for that center to stabilize within that envelope
5. do not import higher-order expansion assumptions

---

## 8. Bottom line

Primitive stable worlds are the first stable semantic field of Varoli.

They are where:
- primitive classes
- centered balance
- stable world identity
- and readable reusable meaning

first become systematically available.
