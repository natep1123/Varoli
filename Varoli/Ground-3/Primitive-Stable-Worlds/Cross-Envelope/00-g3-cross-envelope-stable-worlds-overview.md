---

## 1. Purpose

This folder is a placeholder for cross-envelope primitive stable worlds.

A cross-envelope primitive stable world still has primitive `VCV` structure, but the outer substrates differ.

Examples:
- `aVu`
- `eVo`
- `iRa`

These are structurally important, but they are not mapped here yet because the first priority is to establish the same-envelope baseline cleanly.

---

## 2. Why this is separate

Cross-envelope worlds should not be mixed into the same-envelope baseline too early.

Separating them helps preserve:
- clarity
- learnability
- comparison
- a clean baseline before interpretation becomes denser

The same-envelope map answers:
> what kinds of worlds can stabilize inside one substrate envelope?

The cross-envelope map will answer:
> what happens when a centered world holds different substrate data on its left and right sides?

These are related, but not the same question.

---

## 3. Current status

Cross-envelope worlds are recognized as:
- structurally relevant
- likely important
- necessary for later generative work

But they remain exploratory at this stage.

The inventory is not yet formalized here.

---

## 4. Future work

This folder should eventually map:
- all mixed-envelope primitive VCV worlds
- grouped in a way that remains readable
- with notes on which feel strong, weak, usable, or merely structural

That work should happen only after the same-envelope baseline is established and used.

---

## 5. Bottom line

Cross-envelope primitive stable worlds are the next stable-world field.

They are preserved here as a deliberate next step, not ignored and not prematurely normalized.
