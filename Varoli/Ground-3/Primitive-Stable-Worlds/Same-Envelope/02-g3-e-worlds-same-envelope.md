## 1. Envelope anchor

`e` = individuation  
The process by which something becomes distinct and maintains distinctness.

These worlds are built from:
- individuation on the left
- centered force or operator
- individuation on the right

## 3. Quick read

In this file:
- the outer substrate is always `e`
- the center changes
- the stable world changes according to the centered primitive

Examples:
- `eYe` = canonical collapse-centered self-world
- `eDe` = self organized around resistance
- `eBe` = self organized around emergence

## 4. Reminder

These are primitive same-envelope stable worlds only.
They are not directional forms, expanded stable worlds, or cross-envelope worlds.
