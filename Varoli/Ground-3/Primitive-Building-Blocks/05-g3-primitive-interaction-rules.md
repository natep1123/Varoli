# 05 — Varoli G3 Primitive Interaction Rules

*Last Updated: March 28, 2026*

**Status:** Working reference  
**Layer:** Ground 3 / Primitive-Building-Blocks  
**Authority:** Primitive generation rules  
**Scope:** Defines the immediate interaction rules governing primitive-level generation in Varoli.

---

## 1. Purpose

This file records the immediate rules needed to generate and validate primitive-level forms.

It does not attempt to cover the full higher-order grammar.
It exists to answer:
- what primitive forms are
- how classes interact
- what directionality means
- what counts as primitive, stable, layered, or exploratory

---

## 2. Primitive interaction law

All primitive generation begins from class interaction:

- substrate
- kinetic
- operator

Valid primitive generation must preserve:
- class separation
- invariant function
- readable structure
- directionality

---

## 3. Directionality rules

### 3.1 CV
**Formula:** kinetic + substrate

**Meaning:** force arrives into domain

This is the primary directional form.
It is the active arrival layer.

### 3.2 VC
**Formula:** substrate + kinetic

**Meaning:** domain expresses through force

This is the receptive directional form.
It is not equivalent to CV.

### 3.3 CV and VC are not interchangeable
A reversal in order is a structural change, not a stylistic variant.

---

## 4. Primitive directional forms

Primitive directional forms are the immediate two-character realizations generated by CV and VC interaction.

They are not ordinary verbs in the inherited grammatical sense.
They are directional force-domain forms.

Examples:
- `ve`
- `av`
- `li`
- `or`
- `mu`
- `ud`

---

## 5. Primitive stable worlds

The strongest current primitive stable worlds are attractor-centered forms.

Canonical primitive stable worlds include:
- `aVa`
- `iLi`
- `oRo`
- `uMu`
- `eYe`

These function as stable centered worlds rather than directional forms.

---

## 6. Stable-form rule

Current strongest formulation:

- strict phonemic palindrome is the earliest and best-verified manifestation of stability
- centered balance is the deeper invariant currently understood to govern stable form

At the primitive level, strict attractor-centered palindromic forms remain the clearest validated stable worlds.

---

## 7. Operator rule

`y` introduces superposition and governs collapse.

At primitive level:
- it may appear in directional forms
- it may appear at stable center
- it may suspend in terminal position

It must remain operator class in all positions.

---

## 8. Qualification rule

Qualification is real in current Varoli development, but it is not part of the smallest primitive generation layer in the same sense as CV, VC, and primitive stable worlds.

At this layer:
- note that suffix qualification exists
- do not treat every qualified form as primitive
- classify layered forms separately from primitive two-character forms

---

## 9. Glottal-boundary rule

The glottal stop is not part of the primitive inventory.

If used:
- it functions as an exploratory derivational boundary marker
- it does not add force
- it does not add domain
- it does not add operator behavior

Do not treat glottal-boundary forms as primitive forms.

---

## 10. Validation checklist

When checking a primitive-level form, ask:

1. What class is each symbol?
2. Is the form CV, VC, stable world, operator form, layered form, or exploratory?
3. Does any primitive change class?
4. Does directionality remain readable?
5. Is the reading derivable without hidden mechanics?

If any answer fails, do not approve the form as valid primitive structure.

---

## 11. Classification guide

### 11.1 Primitive
- raw substrate
- raw kinetic
- raw operator
- CV directional form
- VC directional form
- canonical primitive stable world

### 11.2 Derived but not primitive
- layered verbs
- glottal-boundary forms
- expanded stable worlds
- multi-unit chains

### 11.3 Exploratory
- unlicensed compressed forms
- unverified non-phonetic stable worlds
- new operator proposals
- forms requiring hidden class shift

---

## 12. Bottom line

Primitive interaction in Varoli is simple in principle:

- Consonant + Vowel = force into domain
- Vowel + Consonant = domain through force
- center-stabilized world
- operator-governed unresolvedness

Everything more complex must still reduce back to these conditions.
