## 1. Class definition

`y` is the **operator**.

It is not a substrate.
It is not a kinetic.
It acts on structural relations by introducing superposition and governing collapse.

Varoli currently has one established operator.

## 3. Inventory

### `y` — Superposition-Collapse Operator
**Class:** operator  
**Invariant function:** context-resolved collapse through superposition  
**Short description:** marks a position as unresolved and governed by structural collapse  
**Not:** substrate, kinetic, domain, force, or default vowel

---

## 4. Positional behavior

### 4.1 In vowel position
Marks an unresolved substrate position.
The structure constrains possible collapse.

### 4.2 In consonant position
Marks the active event of collapse within structure.

### 4.3 At the center of a stable form
Marks the attractor-state as collapse event rather than substance.
This is the special structural role seen in `eYe`.

### 4.4 In terminal position
Suspends resolution.
The position remains unresolved rather than closing or projecting normally.

---

## 5. Structural rules

### 5.1 `y` remains operator class everywhere
Context may change how the operator functions.
Context may not turn it into a substrate or kinetic.

### 5.2 `y` does not add domain
It does not supply a substrate.

### 5.3 `y` does not add force
It does not behave like a consonant quality of motion.

### 5.4 `y` must remain structurally constrained
Collapse is context-driven, not freely chosen.

---

## 6. Current strongest uses

- elemental collapse-centered form: `eYe`
- terminal suspension in chaining
- Yemu compression and center-outward structure
- operator-mediated unresolvedness in derived contexts

---

## 7. Current cautions

Do not treat `y` as:
- generic ambiguity
- a hidden vowel
- a hidden consonant
- a second substrate
- a catch-all meaning marker

`y` is powerful because it is narrow.

---

## 8. Reminder

If a reading requires `y` to behave like:
- a domain
- a motion
- a stable thing

then the reading is invalid or underived.
