*Last Updated: March 28, 2026*

**Status:** Working reference

**Scope:** Defines the five substrate primitives of Varoli in compact reference form.

---

## 1. Class definition

Vowels are **substrates**.

A substrate defines an ontological domain or condition.
It is not a force.
It does not act.
It is the condition within which force may act, appear, persist, resonate, descend, or resolve.

## 3. Inventory

### `u` — Voidlight
**Class:** substrate  
**Invariant function:** undifferentiated origin  
**Short description:** the substrate prior to distinction, relation, form, individuation, and field  
**Not:** field, relation, individuation, or stable form

### `i` — Air
**Class:** substrate  
**Invariant function:** condition of appearance  
**Short description:** the condition in which anything may appear or be perceived  
**Not:** origin, relation, individuation, or stable form

### `a` — Water
**Class:** substrate  
**Invariant function:** relational resonance  
**Short description:** the medium through which distinct things affect and connect to one another  
**Not:** origin, field, individuation, or stable form

### `e` — Fire
**Class:** substrate  
**Invariant function:** individuation  
**Short description:** the process of becoming distinct and maintaining distinctness  
**Not:** origin, field, relation, or stable form

### `o` — Earth
**Class:** substrate  
**Invariant function:** stable addressable form  
**Short description:** bounded, referenceable, enduring form  
**Not:** origin, field, relation, or individuation as process

## 4. Quick contrast set

- `u` = prior to distinction
- `i` = condition of appearance
- `a` = relation between distinct things
- `e` = becoming distinct
- `o` = stable thingness

---

## 5. Structural notes

### 5.1 Substrates do not overlap
A valid form depends on the distinction between the five substrates remaining clear.

### 5.2 Substrates are not kinetics
A vowel does not contribute motion by itself.

### 5.3 Substrates may frame
In perspective-marker position, a substrate may frame a resolution unit.
It remains a substrate when doing so.

### 5.4 Substrates may center stable worlds
In stable forms, substrates appear as the surrounding domain of the attractor or centered structure.
The substrate still defines the domain, not the force.

---

## 6. Common primitive roles

### In CV forms
The substrate is the arrival domain of force.

### In VC forms
The substrate expresses through force.

### In stable worlds
The substrate defines the domain in which the attractor-centered structure stabilizes.

---

## 7. Reminder

A vowel never becomes:
- a kinetic
- an operator
- an independent action

If a reading requires that, the reading is invalid or underived.
