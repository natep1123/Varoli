# Structural Note: CV/VC Directionality in the Diving System

*This note documents the relationship between primitive verb directionality and the diving mechanics. It is a structural observation, not a new rule. The behavior described here was already present in the system and is now named explicitly.*

---

## The Observation

The CV/VC distinction in the primitive verb system encodes structural directionality: orientation toward center or away from it.

- CV (force → element): arrival, entry, descent, compression toward center
- VC (element → force): departure, exit, ascent, expansion away from center

This directionality is operative throughout the diving system.

---

## Zone Declarations

Each zone declaration is palindromic because it contains both directions simultaneously.

*av-e-va*:
- *av* (VC) — water expressing through vitality. Departure direction. The element receiving the diver.
- *va* (CV) — vitality projecting into water. Arrival direction. The diver projecting into the element.

The declaration is registered upon entry and again before departure. The direction changes. The word does not. The palindromic structure holds both orientations in a single form.

This is true of all three zone declarations: *av-e-va*, *um-e-mu*, *il-e-li*. Each contains its own arrival and departure as VC and CV morphemes on opposite sides of the self-marker *e*.

---

## Gate Codes

The gate codes make directionality explicit at the morpheme level.

In *av-y-mu*:
- *av* (VC) — departure from the Liminal. Water-vitality releasing. The current domain expressing outward.
- *y* — the collapse event. The moment of superposition between two domains.
- *mu* (CV) — arrival at the Black Sun. Depth-force descending into void. The next domain receiving the diver.

The gate code is the structural junction between departure and arrival, with the operator as the collapse event that separates release from crystallization. The left morpheme faces outward (VC). The right morpheme faces inward (CV). The operator sits between them.

This pattern holds for all gate codes in the diving system.

---

## The Dive Poem

The descent side of the dive poem moves from surface toward singularity. This is CV motion: force arriving at element, compression toward center. Language shortens. Structure compresses. The direction is inward.

The ascent side moves from singularity toward surface. This is VC motion: element expressing outward through force, expansion away from center. Structure rebuilds. Language lengthens. The direction is outward.

The dive poem's bilateral symmetry around the Y crossing is not decorative. It is the structural encoding of CV/VC directionality at the scale of an entire composition.
